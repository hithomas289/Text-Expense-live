The Receipt Problem

You have receipts everywhere. Wallet. Car. Desk drawer. Email inbox. Camera roll. Random pockets. Shoebox labeled "2024 taxes."

Some are faded thermal paper that's become unreadable. Some got washed with laundry. Some you photographed but can't find the photos. Some you definitely had but now can't locate.

Tax time arrives. You need to prove $15,000 in business expenses. But receipts are scattered, missing, or illegible. So you claim what you can remember and pay taxes on the rest.

The IRS doesn't accept "I know I bought that" as documentation. No receipt means no deduction. Every lost receipt is money you can't get back.

Why Receipt Management Matters

Thermal receipts fade fast

Most store receipts use thermal paper that becomes unreadable within 2 years. Sometimes faster. Digital copies never fade.

Physical receipts get lost

Wallets get lost. Papers get tossed. Pockets go through the wash. Digital backups can't disappear or get destroyed.

IRS requires documentation

Claiming $10,000 in business expenses? IRS might ask for proof. Having organized digital receipts protects deductions.

Tax preparation is easier

Give accountant organized records instead of chaos. Faster preparation, smaller bill, fewer questions.

Audit protection

If IRS audits your return, you need receipts. Digital organization means every receipt is accessible, not scattered in boxes.

Common Receipt Mistakes

Keeping only physical copies

Thermal paper fades. Coffee spills happen. Papers get lost. Physical-only storage fails eventually.

Photos scattered everywhere

Camera roll has 5,000 photos including maybe 200 receipts you can't identify. Not organized, not useful for taxes.

Shoebox method

Physical storage requires space, sorting, and hoping nothing gets damaged. Still need to digitize for accountants anyway.

Waiting to organize

Planning to scan everything quarterly? Never happens. Life gets busy, receipts pile up, documentation gets missed.

No backup system

Storing receipts only on your phone? Lose phone, lose all documentation. Cloud backup protects everything.

Common Questions

What happens to my receipt images?

Stored in secure cloud backup with bank-level encryption. You control when things get deleted. We never access your receipts.

How long are receipts kept?

Indefinitely until you delete them. IRS requires 3-year retention for most expenses, but we don't auto-delete anything.

Can I access receipts from multiple devices?

Yes. Excel reports work on any device. Download links to receipt images work from phone, computer, tablet - wherever you need them.

What if receipt is unclear or blurry?

We process it anyway and let you know if details are uncertain. You can retake photo or manually enter information.

Do I need to organize receipts myself?

No. We extract details and categorize automatically. You just confirm categories - takes 10 seconds per receipt.

What about receipts from before I started?

Text old receipt photos anytime. If you have physical receipts from earlier in the year, photograph and text them. Better late than never.
