How Consultants Manage Expenses (Without Losing Their Weekends)

Consultants travel, eat out with clients, buy tools, pay for subscriptions, and work across multiple cities. That means lots of receipts. Lots of categories. Lots of reimbursement rules.

But consultants don't have extra hours for admin work.

You bill for client deliverables - not for sorting receipts on Sunday evening.

Why Consultants Hate Expense Tracking

Too many meals + coffees: Client lunches, working dinners, coffee shop meetings. Every week brings a dozen meal receipts.

Too many Uber/Lyft rides: Airport to hotel, hotel to client office, office to dinner. Multiple rides daily when traveling.

Too much travel: Flights, hotels, parking, tolls, baggage fees. Every business trip generates 10+ receipts.

Monthly subscriptions everywhere: Software tools, cloud services, research platforms, communication apps. Recurring expenses that need tracking.

Tight deadlines: No time during the week to organize receipts. It all piles up for the weekend.

Reimbursement policies differ by client: Some want detailed reports, others need QuickBooks imports, some require photos attached.

Spreadsheets take forever: Manually entering merchant, date, amount, category, project code - for 50+ monthly expenses.

Expense apps require too much effort: Download, create account, learn interface, categorize manually. Most consultants quit after two weeks.

Receipts get lost during travel: Paper stuffed in briefcases, jacket pockets, car consoles. By month-end, half are missing or illegible.

No wonder most consultants track expenses only before reimbursements - and panic every time.

  • Ground transportation - Uber, Lyft, taxis, public transit
  • Phone & internet - Business portion of bills

  • Marketing & business development - Website, ads, networking events

Common Questions from Consultants

"Can I separate expenses by client or project?"

Yes. Add a quick note when texting the receipt: "Client A," "Project Phoenix," "Q4 Engagement." Filter and export by client when needed.

"What about international travel receipts?"

Works globally. The system reads receipts in multiple currencies and languages. Perfect for consultants who travel internationally.

"Do I need different categories for billable vs. non-billable?"

Use custom tags or notes. Mark receipts as "Billable" or "Internal." Export filtered reports based on what you need to submit.

"Can I export to QuickBooks or Expensify?"

Download your Excel file and import to any accounting software. Works with QuickBooks, Expensify, Concur, SAP, or whatever your client requires.

"What if I forget to track a receipt?"

Text the photo whenever you find it. The system extracts the original date from the receipt, so it's organized correctly even if you text it weeks later.

"How do I handle per diem vs. actual expenses?"

Track actual expenses by texting receipts. If your client uses per diem, you still have records for tax deductions on anything beyond the per diem amount.

Consultant-Specific Pro Tips

Text receipts before you leave the location: Airport gate, restaurant exit, hotel checkout, Uber ride. Don't wait till you're back at the hotel.

Use voice notes for client/project tags: After texting the receipt, send a 3-second voice note: "Acme Corp, Q4 strategy project." Faster than typing.

Set a weekly reminder: Friday afternoon, download your weekly expense report. Quick review ensures nothing's missed before the weekend.

Separate personal and business from day one: Only text business receipts. Keep everything clean for reimbursements and taxes.

Track mileage separately: Note business mileage when driving your personal car. Standard mileage rate can save thousands yearly.
