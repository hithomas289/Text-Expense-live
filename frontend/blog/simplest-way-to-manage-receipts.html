Why Receipt Management Is Never Simple

Every system you've tried eventually fails:

Shoebox method: Sounds simple until you need to find one specific receipt among hundreds. Now you're dumping everything on the floor, sorting by date, squinting at faded ink, and realizing half your receipts are illegible.

Filing folders: Works great if you actually remember to file receipts every day. But you don't. So they pile up in your bag, on your desk, in your car. When you finally sit down to organize them, you've got 50 receipts to sort and you've forgotten what half of them were for.

Scanning apps: Download app, create account, scan receipt, crop image, categorize, review, save. Then do it again. And again. After the third receipt, you're already annoyed. By the tenth, you're back to shoving receipts in your wallet.

The problem isn't you. The problem is that every system requires too much effort to maintain.

What "Simple" Actually Means

Simple means you can do it while standing at the register. While sitting in your car. While walking out of the store.

Simple means:

  • No apps to download or learn
  • No filing systems to maintain
  • No categories to set up
  • No weekly catchup sessions

You get a receipt. You handle it immediately. It's organized. Done.

That's the only system that actually works long-term. Because the simpler it is, the more likely you'll actually do it.
